NOIRLEANS, 1935. A city built on a swamp and run like one. The Great Depression
has been grinding for six years — bread lines past jazz clubs, men in good suits
sleeping in doorways. Prohibition ended two years ago but the men who got rich off
it didn't go anywhere. The port employs half the city and the mob employs the other
half, and there's significant overlap.

Six weeks ago, Governor Howie Short was shot dead in the State Capitol by a man
named Emil Voss, who was himself shot dead on the spot. Short's machine is still
running — angry, leaderless, looking for someone to pay. The Voss family is not
having a good autumn. Old money is quietly relieved. Nobody says so out loud.

Race runs under everything. Always has.
