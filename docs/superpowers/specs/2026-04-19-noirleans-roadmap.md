# Noirleans Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-04-19
**Status:** Approved

---

## The Game

**Noirleans** is a text-based noir detective game set in 1935 — a city built on jazz, corruption, and the residue of Prohibition. Every case is procedurally generated: the victim, the suspects, the alibis, the lies. The player is a detective with a partner whose past is as tangled as the city's, working cases that feel like they could have actually happened here.

The core experience is **conversation under pressure** — talking to suspects who have secrets worth keeping, collecting evidence that points in more than one direction, and eventually making an accusation you have to live with. The city doesn't forgive wrong arrests. Neither does your partner.

The long-term vision is a **1930s radio drama you can step inside** — voice-acted by AI, each character with a distinct voice and way of speaking, populated by a city that remembers what you've done, open to more than one detective at a time.

---

## The Road to v1.0

v1.0 ships when a player can finish five cases with the same partner and want to start a sixth. Three milestones get there.

---

### Milestone 1 — Clean Floor

The playthrough agents currently in development stress-test every case for world consistency bugs: contradictory NPC statements, missed appointments, routine violations, jailbreak vulnerabilities. This milestone is complete when the agents run cleanly and the issues they surface are fixed. The game should be embarrassment-free before anything else gets built.

Deliverables: playthrough agents shipped, consistency bugs resolved, trial system stable end-to-end, test coverage solid.

---

### Milestone 2 — Every Case Feels New

The replayability problem is a case variety problem. Right now cases share too much DNA — similar locations, similar NPC dynamics, similar evidence trails. This milestone makes each generated case feel like it happened in a different corner of Noirleans.

Deliverables: expanded archetypes and themes, NPC speech styles that are genuinely distinct and enforced, crime scenes that feel specific to the case, red herrings that actually mislead, fixed-location NPCs (Lou, the DA) that react to your case history.

---

### Milestone 3 — The Partner Has a Life

The dark past arc is the emotional core of the game, but it's one-shot. After resolution, the partner goes quiet. This milestone makes the partner relationship a reason to keep playing — they remember past cases, their dynamic with the player evolves, and their presence makes each new case feel personal.

Deliverables: partner references prior cases in briefings and banter, relationship stages have meaningful mechanical weight, a second partner arc activates after dark past resolution, wrong arrests leave a scar in the partnership.

---

## The Horizon

Three long bets. None of them are on the immediate roadmap, but every decision made before v1.0 should leave the door open for them.

---

### A City That Remembers

Right now Noirleans resets between cases. The long bet is a city that accumulates — where solving (or botching) the Fontaine murder six months ago means something when a new body turns up in the same neighborhood. Faction power shifts based on who gets arrested. Corrupt officials you exposed are gone; the ones you missed are more entrenched. Lou at the bar has an opinion about your track record. The city is a character, not a backdrop.

---

### More Than One Detective

A shared Noirleans where multiple players work different cases in the same city at the same time — and occasionally collide. Witnesses who've already been interviewed by another detective. Crime scenes that overlap. A city where two investigators can end up on opposite sides of the same corruption. Not a multiplayer game in the traditional sense — more like a city that's genuinely in motion.

---

### The Radio Drama

The ceiling for Noirleans is a game you could play with your eyes closed. Every character speaks with a distinct AI voice shaped by their accent, class, and decade — a Creole dock worker sounds nothing like the DA. The interface disappears entirely: you speak, they speak back, the city sounds like it's happening around you. It plays like a 1930s radio serial where you're the detective and nothing is scripted.
